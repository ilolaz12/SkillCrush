Hi, my name is Drew and I have some advice for you!

First Off - Accept That This Stuff Works

If you're anything like me, you can get in the weeds pretty quickly when you're learning a new skill or set of skills,
but you don't yet understand how it works. Here's my advice....it works - Full Stop. I promise you, you'll understand
the "why" and "how" further down the road.

In the meantime, just go through the lessons, do the exercises, and you'll be fine.

Feeling Stupid is the Smartest Thing You Can Do

This is a big one. Allow yourself to feel completely and utterly lost. Allow yourself to feel like you're never 'gonna get it',
like you're never going to be able to make it through the class, like you're going to be stuck driving your crap-car to your
crap-job until the end of time. All perfectly normal feelings.

So, just prepare to feel like a damned fool. Cuz it's gonna happen (unless you're some sort of wunderkind). You're probably
not 'gonna sail right through this' like you did 9th-grade Algebra (or whatever). If you do, rock on. But that would make
you the exception.

The rest of us are going get to a point, (sooner than later) that may make you wonder how you ever accomplished anything in
your life, because you're gonna feel dumb.


Dumb, dumb, dumb.

Not to worry!
You are, in fact, not dumb.
You are just as smart as the rest of us.

That feeling of all-consuming idiot-hood will come and go sooooo many times during the lessons, because it's exactly how
you're supposed to feel - It's how you know that you're actually learning.

(I mean, you made it this far, right?)

In my very humble opinion, if you already knew how to do all this "coding stuff", you would feel smart and the classes would
be easy. Simple even. But, if you already knew how to do this stuff, you wouldn't be learning, would you?

So, if feeling smart does not equal learning, that would mean feeling dumb does not equal the opposite of learning...

And, presto! Ipso-facto, ergo, vis-a-vis and all that...Point is, if you apply the those transitive properties from 9th-grade Algebra
to the previous statement then feeling dumb is what makes you smarter, because that's the only emotional state that prepares
your mind to digest new information.

Now get out there and go make an ass of yourself!

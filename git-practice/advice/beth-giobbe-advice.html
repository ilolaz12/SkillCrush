Hi, my name is Beth and I have some advice for you!

Computers are not smart. You are smart.

Computer programming is the process of reducing something you know how to do into steps so small and orderly that even a machine can do them. The hard part is becoming conscious of what you do unconsciously. The computer will do exactly what you tell it to do, in the order you tell it to, not what you're thinking. It can't read your mind; it has no intuition or common sense. It takes everything literally. You are much smarter. 😀

Error messages are your friends

Oh, no! I got an error! Don't panic. Part of learning a programming language is making all the errors and learning how to catch them. It does get easier.

Most error messages tell you what line number the error is on and what type of error it is. They may even tell you exactly how to fix them! Sometimes the error is actually on the previous line — maybe missing punctuation. If you still don't know what is wrong, google the error message. Someone somewhere has had that problem before.

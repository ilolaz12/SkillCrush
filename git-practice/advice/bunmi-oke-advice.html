Realize and accept this is no quick journey. It's normal your learning will take time. (Hint: It's lifelong.) This is as well a cue that you should strive for depth, not haste.

You don't want to be some Senior Programmer with yawning gaps in your grasp of rudiments that it all slaps a huge question mark on your foundational diligence.

Your future programmer self will thank you for taking the time and trouble to be thorough in your learning, not skimping on fundamentals, not glossing over basics.

Binge Build is the Watchword.

Trust me, you can stare at a learning video all day, soak up all that is being taught like a sponge, and return to the same resource a couple weeks later only to wonder what in the Greek the tutor is saying.

You will realize programming isn't so much about cramming syntax or, excellent as it sounds, knowing how something is done. It is about doing, plain and simple.

So in your pursuit of learning, retaining stuff and gaining mastery, ask yourself: Why be a passive binge-watcher when I can be an obsessive builder?
